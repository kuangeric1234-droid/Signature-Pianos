Why it matters

Why every piano needs regular tuning.

Pianos go out of tune naturally over time. Changes in temperature, humidity, and season all affect the tension of the strings — and Melbourne's variable climate makes this especially pronounced. Most manufacturers recommend tuning at least twice a year. A piano that hasn't been tuned in years may require a pitch raise before a standard tuning can hold.

What we offer

Four kinds of service.

Whether your piano needs a quick refresh or a complete overhaul, there's a service to suit. Every visit ends with a written report so you know exactly what was done.

Standard tuning

A full pitch assessment and tuning to concert pitch A440. Suitable for pianos tuned within the last 12 months.

From $120

Pitch raise

Required when a piano has dropped significantly below concert pitch. Two-pass process to safely bring strings back to tension before fine tuning.

From $180

Regulation & voicing

Adjusting the action mechanics and hammer felt to restore touch and tone. Recommended every 5–10 years or when the piano feels unresponsive.

From $280

Full service & inspection

Comprehensive assessment covering tuning, regulation, voicing, and a written condition report. Ideal for recently purchased or inherited pianos.

From $380

FAQ

Common questions.

At least twice a year for a regularly played instrument. Melbourne's seasonal humidity changes can cause pianos to drift more noticeably than in more stable climates. Pianos that haven't been tuned in several years will likely need a pitch raise first.
A pitch raise is required when a piano has dropped significantly below concert pitch A440. If strings are tuned in one pass from too low, they'll pull other strings out as they're tensioned. A pitch raise brings the whole instrument up gradually first, then a fine tuning follows. We'll assess this on arrival.
A standard tuning takes approximately 60–90 minutes. A pitch raise plus tuning takes 90–120 minutes. A full service and inspection may take half a day.
